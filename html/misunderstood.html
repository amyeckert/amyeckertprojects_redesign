Misunderstood buildings

What began as a way to re-imagine what goes on inside other people’s homes, namely, famous buildings and architect-designed homes of the wealthy, has become an exploration of the supposedly stable boundary between the rationality of architectural form —and by implication architectural photography, versus the irrationality of imagination and emotion. I am led by a sense of play. In this collage series I combine my own images with found photographs taken from pages of architecture books. I physically remove the structure from these landscapes and overlay the remaining scene on top of my own, filling up the voids with other possibilities. I think of it as renovating.

In architecture, a folly is an extravagant, useless, or fanciful building, or one that appears to be something other than what it is. In their book Follies, Grottoes & Garden Buildings, Headley and Meulenkamp define a folly as a "Misunderstood Building".
